NASA Packet Dissector Project (2021)

Problem statement

During a launch, or even when the Space Launch System is at rest on the launchpad, hundreds of onboard sensors are sending a constant stream of mission-critical data to Mission Control in the firing room. NASA engineers needed a way to verify that this data wasn't being lost or corrupted in transit.

Our solution

My internship partner, Alan Lu, and I built a packet dissection and verification tool that sits between the rocket and the firing room and triple-checks all the incoming data and outgoing commands for correctness. We collaborated remotely to successfully complete our project and present it to the engineering team. This is the second piece of software I've written that will be running in the firing room when Artemis I launches.
