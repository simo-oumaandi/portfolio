NASA Packet Dissector Project (2021)

Click here for the project abstract

Problem statement: During a launch, or even when the Space Launch System (SLS) is at rest on the launchpad, hundreds of onboard sensors are sending a constant stream of mission-critical data to Mission Control in the "firing room." NASA engineers needed a way to verify that this data wasn't being lost or corrupted in transit.

Our solution: My internship partner, Alan Lu, and I built a packet dissection and verification tool that sits between the rocket and the firing room and triple-checks all the incoming data and outgoing commands for correctness.
