The DadBot 3009 (2021)

Problem statement

My dad golfs at a course where tee times are released at a certain hour, a certain number of days in advance. His extremely competitive golfing buddies hover over their mousepads, ready to click the best times as soon as the clock strikes 7am. Due to click reflex, network speed, and other factors, Dad rarely got decent times--which, in the Arizona summers, means four-hour rounds in hundred-plus-degree heat.

Our solution

I built a little web bot that, when run before 7am on the appropriate day, jumps onto the course website and snatches a good time in an average of 0.1-0.15 seconds: significantly faster than the average human click reflex of 0.25 seconds. The DadBot has scored my dad and his buddies some pretty prime times, and no one knows his secret... so keep it on the down-low, okay?
