RING-Bearer dVPN Framework (2021)

Problem statement

Decentralized VPNs, or dVPNs, are becoming an increasingly popular solution to security issues within the classical centralized VPN model. dVPNs rely on networks of volunteer node users, but being a node user is difficult: you can only lend bandwidth to one dVPN provider at once, and you have almost no control over the type of traffic routed through your node machine.

Our solution

The RING dVPN framework is an ongoing research project at Northwestern University that aims to create a centralized pool of node users and to give those users control over when and how dVPN providers use their bandwidth. My project partner Jordan Zax and I made additions to RING that allow node users to customize their network policies by time of day and traffic routing preferences and enforce those decisions to three dVPN providers: Mysterium, Tachyon, and Sentinel.
